The Itinerary

A guided long-weekend hike with Much Better Adventures. We'll be trekking roughly 15-20km each day over 3 days, staying in mountain huts.

Pace & Terrain Demanding terrain, steady pace. Good fitness required.
Day 1 Preview 3-4hrs • 7km

The UTMB Factor

Our trip lands on the same weekend as the 100-mile Ultra Trail du Mont Blanc (~50k visitors). Good news: We are hiking anti-clockwise to avoid crowds, so the trail itself will remain tranquil.

After meeting your hosts at Geneva Airport, you'll be driven through the Mont Blanc tunnel to Plampincieux, just outside Courmayeur on the Italian side. From there you'll ascend into the Val Ferret heading towards the Italian-Swiss border, with inspiring views of Mont Blanc, Dent du Geants, and the Grandes Jorasses massif. You'll reach your mountain refuge high up in the valley in time for a hearty dinner.

Mountain Hut · Mixed DormLunch + Dinner

Start the morning with an invigorating ascent up to Grand Col Ferret (2,536m), crossing from Italy into Switzerland. This is one of the most challenging passes on the TMB route — a long steady climb with incredible views of Val Ferret, Mont Dolent and the Swiss side of the valley. From the top you'll take the more gradual, long descent down to La Fouly village, stopping at a traditional dairy farm with a chance to sample local produce. From La Fouly you'll be driven to Champex for the night.

Mountain Hut · Mixed DormAll MealsHardest Day

Hike up and over the mountains via the classic Bovine route to Trient. This winding forest trail leads past mountain streams to the Bovine Refuge and the high alpages, with spectacular views of the Rhone Valley. Make sure to stop for a slice of the best cake the region has to offer! From the top you'll descend through forest to the Col de la Forclaz, enjoy deep valley views from a boardwalk looking down on Trient and its famous pink church, then a short section down to the village for the night.

Mountain Hut · Mixed DormAll Meals

Your last long ascent: Col de Balme (2,190m), rewarded with an expansive vista down the Chamonix Valley towards Mont Blanc and the Aiguille du Midi. Then traverse to L'Aiguillettes des Posettes for another jaw-dropping viewpoint, and descend through glades and forest to Tre-le-Champ. Hike usually finishes around 15:30 — local bus back to central Chamonix for a quick celebration drink. Shared bus to Geneva Airport departs ~17:00.
